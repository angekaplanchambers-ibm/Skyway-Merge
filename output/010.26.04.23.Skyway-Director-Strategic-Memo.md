# Skyway Merge Memo - Strategic Product Framing

**Date**: 2026-04-23  
**Audience**: Director-level product, design, and engineering leaders  
**Purpose**: Provide a concise strategic framing for socializing the Skyway effort across partner teams and leadership conversations.

## Executive Summary

The Skyway Merge is a product-clarity initiative aimed at reducing user confusion across overlapping Terraform features while preserving their distinctness.

This is an opportunity to create a coherent experience without forcing a premature merger of Search, Explorer View, and Infragraph into a single generalized tool. The goal is to unify the journey and not flatten the product. That means users should experience a more connected path from finding issues,  understanding context and taking action; while each surface remains accountable with a core identity.

The most important question is not "Can these products be combined?" It is "What level of cohesion materially improves user clarity, adoption, and action quality without destroying product focus?"

## The Problem We Are Solving

As Terraform expands, adjacent surfaces increasingly overlap in the user’s mind. Search, Explorer View, and Infragraph all participate in the same end-to-end workflow, but they do so from different angles. That creates a predictable problem:

- users are not always sure where to start
- users do not always understand why one surface is better than another for a given task
- internal teams risk investing in overlap without a shared product model
- platform complexity grows faster than platform clarity

This is now a strategic product problem because confusion at the navigation and mental-model layer weakens adoption, slows time to value, and makes the portfolio feel more fragmented than it actually is.

## Strategic Thesis

The emerging thesis from this work is straightforward:

**Terraform should feel like one connected product journey across multiple specialized surfaces.**

That implies five strategic positions:

1. The user journey should be continuous across discovery, context, and remediation.
2. The products in that journey should remain differentiated by core job.
3. Navigation and handoff patterns should be more consistent than they are today.
4. Product success should be measured by user clarity and task completion, not by how merged the interfaces appear.

## What the Skyway Merge Is, Strategically

The Skyway Merge is a cross-surface product strategy with three goals:

- create a clearer platform mental model
- reduce friction between adjacent workflows
- establish reusable operating patterns for future Terraform surfaces

In that framing, Skyway is a mechanism for portfolio coherence. It gives leadership a way to make smarter decisions about where standardization is valuable, where product boundaries should remain intact, and how future capabilities should fit into the ecosystem.

This matters beyond the current three previously-noted features. If Terraform continues to add more insight, governance, graph, remediation, and operations capabilities, the absence of a shared product model will compound over time. Skyway is the chance to set that model before the fragmentation becomes structural.

## Why This Matters Now

The current moment is favorable for this work for three reasons.

### 1. Portfolio overlap is increasing

These product features already intersect in user workflows and that overlap will not disappear on its own. Without a clearer strategy, teams will keep solving adjacent problems in locally rational but globally inconsistent ways.

### 2. User confusion is an adoption risk

When users are unsure where to go, they hesitate, duplicate their efforts, or choose the wrong path. That weakens confidence in the overall platform and makes advanced capabilities harder to discover.

### 3. Leadership needs a scalable rubric

This effort provides a decision framework leadership can reuse: what should be standardized, what should remain separate, and what user outcome should justify integration work.

## Strategic Direction

The recommended direction is **incremental cohesion with explicit product boundaries**.

In practical terms, that means:

- one shared journey model across surfaces
- clearer role definition for each surface
- stronger handoffs and context carryover between surfaces
- shared language and wayfinding patterns
- no forced full merger unless evidence later shows that a merger would improve user outcomes

This is a more durable strategy than choosing between total separation and total consolidation. It allows the organization to improve the user experience now while keeping options open for deeper integration later.

## Alignment Goals

Emphasize a small set of product-level messages.

### Message 1: We are unifying the journey, not collapsing the product features

The user should experience one coherent Terraform path, but each surface should still have a clear reason to exist.

### Message 2: Clarity and definition is the primary outcome

The goal is not visual consistency for its own sake. The goal is to reduce ambiguity about where to go, why, and what happens next.

### Message 3: Cohesion is a platform capability

Shared navigation, and context-preservation patterns are not design polish. They are product infrastructure that will make future capabilities easier to introduce and easier to understand.

### Message 4: This creates a repeatable decision model

Skyway is useful not only because it improves the current experience, but because provides a method for evaluating adjacent product feature overlaps going forward.

## Risks and Tradeoffs

The main strategic risk is overcorrecting in either direction.

If the organization pushes too far toward merger, it may erase useful product differentiation and create a larger but less legible experience.

If it stays too fragmented, users will continue to absorb the cost of internal product boundaries.

The right tradeoff is to make the transitions feel unified while keeping each product accountable for a distinct value proposition.

There is also an organizational tradeoff. Cross-surface coherence requires teams to align on shared patterns and success metrics, which can feel slower than shipping within a single product boundary. But that coordination cost is lower than the long-term cost of unmanaged overlap.

## What Success Looks Like

At a strategic level:

- users can explain the difference between the surfaces in plain language
- users choose the right starting point with less hesitation
- users move between discovery, context, and remediation with fewer dead ends
- teams can describe the experience as one system with specialized parts
- leadership can evaluate future overlaps with a clearer framework

## Recommended Talking Points

- Skyway is a product strategy for reducing cross-surface confusion in Terraform.
- The work is focused on user clarity, not interface consolidation for its own sake.
- Our current direction is to connect the workflow across surfaces while preserving distinct core jobs.
- This gives us a scalable model for future Terraform capabilities, not just a one-time fix for current overlap.
- We should continue validating whether added cohesion improves adoption, confidence, and action quality before committing to deeper consolidation.

## Near-Term Ask

The near-term ask is alignment on the strategic frame:

- agree that the problem is portfolio clarity, not only local UX refinement
- agree that the target state is connected workflow plus clear product roles
- agree to evaluate future integration work against measurable user benefit

If leadership aligns on those points, the rest of the work can proceed with a clearer product mandate and a more stable decision model.
