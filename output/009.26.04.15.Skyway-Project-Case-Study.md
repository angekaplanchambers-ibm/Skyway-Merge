## Introductory Summary: Problem Statement and Process

The core problem identified in the meeting notes is that users and internal teams do not have a clear mental model for when to use Search, Explorer View, or Infragraph. As Terraform evolves into a more consolidated platform, overlap between these surfaces increases risk of confusion, context switching, and reduced efficiency, especially across the shared user journey of finding, understanding, and acting on infrastructure.

The process used to address this was intentionally investigative and design-led. The workshop was framed as a thought exercise to create clarity before platform commitments, not as a predetermined merge plan. The team used explicit decision criteria to evaluate tradeoffs, captured product boundaries, discussed current and ideal future states, and aligned on evidence needed to support leadership direction. This produced a structured foundation for recommendations, wireframe exploration, and leadership share-out.

## Consolidated Summary of Discussions and Outputs

### 1. What was discussed in the meeting

- Why this work is needed now:
  - Leadership anticipates confusion as Search, Explorer View, and Infragraph continue to overlap.
  - The discussion is proactive and design-led, with "do nothing" kept as a valid outcome.
- Shared user journey model:
  - Find/Investigate
  - View/Understand
  - Remediate/Act
- Surface role clarity:
  - Search: discovery and import-oriented remediation workflows, especially for unmanaged resources.
  - Explorer View: high-level managed posture and governance snapshot.
  - Infragraph: relationship context, dependencies, lineage, and blast radius across managed and unmanaged resources.
- Core overlap concern:
  - Users ask "Which tool should I use right now?"
  - Distinction between discovery, deep context, and action is not clear in practice.
- Decision criteria used:
  - Mental model clarity
  - Learnability and cognitive load
  - Power-user efficiency
  - Platform clarity
  - Technical and organizational feasibility
  - Cost versus benefit

### 3. Leadership Share Out summary

From [output/008.26.04.08.Skyway-Leadership-OnePager-Decisions.md](output/008.26.04.08.Skyway-Leadership-OnePager-Decisions.md), the leadership framing emphasizes:

- One connected 3-step journey across surfaces.
- Keep specialized end states distinct while improving cohesion.
- Preserve Explorer and Infragraph role boundaries.
- Sequence user flow as Insight, Decide, Navigate.
- Improve discoverability with predictable handoffs.
- Keep Search and Import tightly coupled for unmanaged remediation.
- Prioritize user clarity over cosmetic full unification.
- Keep portfolio-level scanning separate from organization-level workflow detail.
- Reuse Visibility mental models without forcing users into the dedicated graph page too early.

### 4. Rationale Matrix summary

From [output/007.26.04.08.Skyway-UI-Updates-Rationale-Matrix.md](output/007.26.04.08.Skyway-UI-Updates-Rationale-Matrix.md), decisions are explicitly mapped to meeting evidence and design implications, including:

- Surface-boundary guidance on Organization Overview
- Concrete cross-surface handoff actions
- Cross-organization relationship visibility
- Unmanaged resource type visibility in portfolio coverage
- A direct "Convert to Managed" path linked to Search and Import
- A remediation queue designed for direct action, not just issue review
- Outcome-oriented posture KPIs and synchronized organization-level posture framing
- Fleet-level scanning in portfolio with deeper workflow detail in Organization Overview
- A compact embedded Visibility preview that reuses query, type, and question patterns from the dedicated Visibility page

## Recommended Next Steps

1. Run UXR validation on current wireframes and Storybook flows.
- Test with representative personas for task clarity, route confidence, and handoff success.
- Measure where users still hesitate on "where to go next" across Find, View, and Act stages.
- Specifically test whether the embedded portfolio Visibility preview helps users understand when to stay in portfolio versus when to enter full graph analysis.

2. Define a cross-feature path-to-success map for Registry, Policy, IAM, and Usage.
- Document how Skyway interaction patterns and handoff logic scale into these adjacent product areas.
- Identify reusable navigation, context carryover, and deep-link standards that can be adopted without forcing full feature merger.

3. Expand Search and Import through an explicit Unmanaged-to-Managed Resource conversion path.
- Formalize conversion entry points from portfolio and overview surfaces.
- Preserve context (resource type, organization, workspace, policy state) through the handoff.
- Validate conversion completion and remediation outcome metrics as a core success indicator.

4. Package a leadership decision checkpoint.
- Present UXR findings, path-to-success mapping, and conversion-path feasibility.
- Confirm whether the recommended direction remains incremental cohesion with distinct end states or requires additional consolidation.
- Include a readout on whether fleet-level summary plus organization-level workflow detail is the right split for scaling the experience.
